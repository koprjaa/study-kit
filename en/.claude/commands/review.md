Run phase 4.3 per CLAUDE.md.

KEY: confront the draft from `study/` AGAINST the primary texts in `raw_primary/`. Do NOT treat
the draft as the source of truth — your creation must not validate itself. For every claim in the
draft verify whether it holds against `raw_primary/`.

Always run the three hard checks:
- quote fidelity (verbatim + contiguity; rule 1),
- frequency of "proofs" (grep; rule 9),
- joints, not bricks — a synthetic core joint stands on a concept, not on word-match (rule 8).
And check the bounding of the thesis (rule 8b): is some text the thesis doesn't extend to
"rescued" by a developmental story or an "exception that proves the rule"? If so, that's an
objection — the correct bounding is a counter-case test (precondition absent → effect absent).

Write the review: summary, original contribution, strengths, main objections (description +
requirement + severity), minor points, verdict, and 1 most important sentence. For objections,
show where the draft departs from `raw_primary/`. Write `process/review-report.md`, log it,
propose `/revise`.
